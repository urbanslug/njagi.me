Encrypting and decypting a flash drive

February 22, 2020

Tags:

Note:

  • You need root for most of these commands
  • Replace X with the target drive letter

Encrypting a flash drive that was previously unencrypted

  1. Install cryptsetup
pacman -S cryptsetup
  1. List your USB devices with either
lsblk

or

fdisk -l
  1. Write random data to disk
shred -v -n 1 /dev/sdX

or

dd if=/dev/urandom of=/dev/sdX bs=1M
  1. Set up a new dm-crypt device in LUKS encryption mode
cryptsetup luksFormat /dev/sdX
  1. Set up mapping
cryptsetup luksOpen /dev/sdX flashy
  1. Verify the new virtual block device mapper
ls -arlt /dev/mapper | tail
  1. Format the disk with ext4
mkfs -t ext4 /dev/mapper/flashy
  1. Create a mount location
mkdir /run/mount/flashy
  1. Mount the device your filesystem:
mount /dev/mapper/flashy /run/mount/flashy
  1. Verify the the mapper is properly mounted using the df command:
df -h /run/mount/flashy

Accessing your flash drive later on.

  1. Decrypt the flash drive
cryptsetup luksOpen /dev/sdX flashy

Look it up

ls -arlt /dev/mapper | tail
  1. Create a mount location
mkdir /run/mount/flashy
  1. Mount the flash drive
mount /dev/mapper/flashy /run/mount/flashy
  1. Access your files
cd /run/mount/flashy
  1. Unmount the flash drive
umount /run/mount/flashy
  1. Cleaup
rm -r /run/mount/flashy
  1. Close your flash drive
cryptsetup luksClose flashy
